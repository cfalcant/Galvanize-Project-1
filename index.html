Coffee: America's caffeine of choice

The United States loves coffee. This is evident by the number of coffee-shops, both new and established, including local and national chains, all across the country. As popular as coffee is here in the states, the U.S. ranks 26th in consumption in comparison to all other countries. According to the International Coffee Organization (ICO), the country of Finland and its inhabitants consume approximately 12kg per capita per year. followed by Norway (9.9 kg per capita per year), and then Iceland (9 kg per capita per year). As far as coffee-producing countries, Brazil leads the pack as the country who exports the largest amount of coffee with a staggering 5.7 bilion pounds per year. Below you will find more interesting information about the wonderful drink and the coffee bean itself.

Fun Facts: Coffee

  • International Coffee Day takes place every September 29th
  • The most expensive coffee beans can cost as much as $160 per pound or $30 per brewed cup, sometimes even more
  • Monaco has the largest number of Starbucks branches with an incredible number of 52.08 branches per 1 million inhabitants.
